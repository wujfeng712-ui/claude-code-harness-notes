# s17 · Autonomous Agents — Watch the Board, Claim Your Own Work

> **Motto: Watch the board, claim your own work.**

## The problem this lesson solves

In s15/s16 teammates are passive: they only act when the Lead hands them a task. Once the Lead becomes the single scheduling bottleneck, parallelism is capped. s17 lets teammates **watch the board and claim work themselves** — entering a `WORK → IDLE → SHUTDOWN` lifecycle and reaching into the task graph for work when idle.

## How the mechanism works

Each teammate runs a lifecycle loop:

```
WORK: inbox → LLM → tools →(more tool_use? continue)→(done? → IDLE)
IDLE: poll every 5s → inbox has a message?   → back to WORK
                    → unclaimed task exists?  → claim → back to WORK
                    → idle for 60s?           → SHUTDOWN
```

- In IDLE, `scan_unclaimed_tasks()` finds tasks that are `pending + unowned + can_start` (dependencies satisfied) and claims them automatically.
- After compaction it auto-reinjects `<identity>You are {name}…` so a teammate doesn't "forget who it is" once its context gets trimmed.

## Key insight

- Flipping scheduling from "Lead pushes" to "teammate pulls" removes the Lead as the parallelism bottleneck.
- Autonomy needs an explicit exit condition: SHUTDOWN after 60s with no work, so idle agents don't burn money spinning.
- Identity must be reinjected after compaction — an autonomous agent must always know who it is and what it should claim.
